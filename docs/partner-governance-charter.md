# KWANUS OS — Partner Governance Charter (PartnerGovCharter)
A complete charter defining the long‑term rules, roles, responsibilities, and decision‑making structures that govern partner relationships within the KWANUS Operating System ecosystem.

This document defines:
- governance principles  
- governance bodies  
- decision‑making rules  
- partner obligations  
- permission governance  
- workflow governance  
- compliance governance  
- lifecycle governance  
- escalation paths  
- long‑term partner governance stewardship  

It ensures partner relationships remain accountable, predictable, and aligned with ecosystem integrity.

---

# 1. Governance Philosophy

Partner governance must be:
- transparent  
- enforceable  
- predictable  
- aligned with ecosystem integrity  
- long‑term oriented  
- protective of user safety  

Governance ensures:
- partner accountability  
- workflow reliability  
- permission integrity  
- compliance alignment  
- ecosystem stability  

---

# 2. Governance Principles

## Principle 1 — Accountability
Partners must be accountable for:
- workflows  
- permissions  
- data handling  
- compliance  

## Principle 2 — Transparency
All partner actions must be:
- auditable  
- documented  
- reviewable  

## Principle 3 — Least Privilege
Partners receive:
- only the permissions they need  
- only the workflows they need  
- only the access they need  

## Principle 4 — Predictability
All changes must be:
- versioned  
- documented  
- governed  

## Principle 5 — Sustainability
Partner governance must:
- preserve long‑term viability  
- maintain ecosystem health  
- avoid fragmentation  

---

# 3. Governance Bodies

## 1. Technical Steering Council (TSC)
Responsible for:
- API governance  
- runtime governance  
- technical lifecycle  

## 2. Security & Compliance Office (SCO)
Responsible for:
- permission governance  
- data protection  
- compliance enforcement  
- security audits  

## 3. Extension Review Council (ERC)
Responsible for:
- workflow governance  
- extension governance  
- partner UI governance  

## 4. Council of Global Balance (CGB)
Responsible for:
- ecosystem governance  
- partner tier governance  
- partner expansion governance  
- final decision authority  

---

# 4. Decision‑Making Rules

## Majority Approval Required For:
- new partner onboarding  
- tier upgrades  
- new capabilities  
- new workflows  

## Unanimous Approval Required For:
- new partner tiers  
- new permission classes  
- intelligence workflows  

## CGB Final Authority For:
- partner suspension  
- partner deprecation  
- partner EoS  
- severe violations  

---

# 5. Partner Obligations

Partners must:
- maintain workflow reliability  
- maintain compliance  
- maintain compatibility  
- follow permission rules  
- follow lifecycle rules  
- participate in audits  
- remediate violations  
- provide documentation  

---

# 6. Permission Governance

## Required
- least privilege  
- explicit declaration  
- justification required  
- periodic review  

## Forbidden
- wildcard permissions  
- implicit permissions  
- partner‑defined permissions  

## Governance Bodies
- SCO (primary)  
- TSC (technical validation)  

---

# 7. Workflow Governance

## Required
- idempotency  
- reversibility  
- auditability  
- versioning  

## Forbidden
- unbounded workflow triggers  
- workflows that bypass governance  

## Governance Bodies
- ERC (primary)  
- SCO (security validation)  

---

# 8. Compliance Governance

## Required
- retention compliance  
- deletion compliance  
- audit log completeness  
- annual compliance review  

## Forbidden
- unauthorized data export  
- long‑term data storage  
- external tracking  

## Governance Bodies
- SCO (primary)  
- CGB (oversight)  

---

# 9. Lifecycle Governance

## Required
- lifecycle updates  
- compatibility updates  
- deprecation rules  
- EoS rules  

## Governance Bodies
- TSC (technical lifecycle)  
- ERC (workflow lifecycle)  
- SCO (compliance lifecycle)  
- CGB (ecosystem lifecycle)  

---

# 10. Escalation Paths

## Technical Violations → TSC  
## Security Violations → SCO  
## Compliance Violations → SCO  
## Workflow Violations → ERC  
## Ecosystem Violations → CGB  

Escalation windows:
- critical: 24 hours  
- high: 7 days  
- medium: 30 days  

---

# 11. Long‑Term Partner Governance Stewardship

## Annual Tasks
- full partner governance review  
- update governance rules  
- update compatibility matrix  
- update lifecycle stages  
- publish governance report  

## Stewardship Principles
- protect ecosystem integrity  
- maintain partner trust  
- ensure long‑term viability  
- preserve workflow stability  

---

# 12. Summary

The Partner Governance Charter ensures:
- strong partner accountability  
- predictable governance  
- safe workflows  
- stable permissions  
- long‑term ecosystem integrity  

This is the backbone of the KWANUS OS partner governance system.
